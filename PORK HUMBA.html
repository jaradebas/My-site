Pork Humba

₱160

Description:

Pork Humba is a Visayan-style braised pork belly dish cooked slowly in soy sauce, vinegar, brown sugar, garlic, and fermented black beans (tausi). It’s tender, flavorful, and has a perfect balance of sweet and savory.

Why order it in a restaurant:

  • It takes time to cook properly for tenderness.
  • The blend of flavors is best when cooked by experienced hands.
  • Harder-to-find ingredients like tausi are usually readily available in restaurant kitchens.

Ingredients:

  • 1 kg pork belly or pork hock (pata), cut into chunks
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup vinegar
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup pineapple juice (optional)
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 tablespoon fermented black beans (tausi)
  • 1/2 teaspoon black peppercorns
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 tablespoon cooking oil

Procedure:

  1. Heat oil in a pan and sauté garlic and onion.
  2. Add pork and cook until lightly browned.
  3. Pour in soy sauce, vinegar, and pineapple juice. Let it simmer for 10 minutes.
  4. Add brown sugar, bay leaves, black beans, and peppercorns. Stir well.
  5. Pour in water and let it simmer for 1-2 hours until the pork is tender. Add more water if needed.
  6. Adjust seasoning, then serve with rice.
