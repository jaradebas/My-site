Tinolang Manok

₱130

Description:

Tinolang Manok is a comforting Filipino chicken soup with green papaya (or sayote), malunggay or chili leaves, and ginger. It's known for its light and refreshing broth with a slight gingery kick.

Why order it in a restaurant:

  • Ideal if you want something healthy and light.
  • Restaurants often serve it with rich broth from native chicken, which adds extra depth of flavor.
  • Fresh vegetables and herbs are usually well-prepared.

Ingredients:

  • 1 kg chicken, cut into serving pieces
  • 1 small green papaya, sliced
  • 2 cups malunggay (moringa) or chili leaves
  • 1 thumb-sized ginger, sliced
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 5 cups water or chicken broth
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon cooking oil

Procedure:

  1. Heat oil in a pot and sauté garlic, onion, and ginger.
  2. Add chicken and cook until lightly browned.
  3. Pour in water or chicken broth and bring to a boil. Simmer for 30 minutes.
  4. Add green papaya and cook until tender.
  5. Season with fish sauce.
  6. Add malunggay leaves and simmer for 2 more minutes.
  7. Serve hot with rice.
