Pork Sinigang

₱140

Description:

Sinigang is a sour soup made with pork, vegetables, and a tamarind-based broth. It’s tangy, savory, and incredibly comforting.

Why order it in a restaurant:

  • well-made sinigang has just the right level of sourness, which can be tricky to perfect.
  • Some places offer sinigang variations (like with guava or calamansi).
  • It’s usually served hot and fresh, perfect on a rainy day

Ingredients:

  • 1 kg pork belly or spare ribs
  • 8 cups water
  • 1 medium onion, sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, quartered
  • 1 packet tamarind soup base (or 1 cup fresh tamarind juice)
  • 1 cup radish, sliced
  • 1 cup string beans
  • 1 cup eggplant, sliced
  • 1 bunch kangkong (water spinach)
  • 2 pieces green chili (optional)
  • Fish sauce and salt to taste

Procedure:

  1. In a pot, boil pork in water until tender, removing scum that rises to the surface.
  2. Add onions and tomatoes, then let it simmer for 10 minutes.
  3. Pour in tamarind soup base or fresh tamarind juice. Stir well.
  4. Add radish, string beans, and eggplant. Cook until vegetables are tender.
  5. Season with fish sauce or salt.
  6. Add kangkong and green chili. Simmer for 2 more minutes.
  7. Serve hot with rice.
