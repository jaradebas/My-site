POCHERO

₱130

Description:

Pochero is a Spanish-influenced stew with tender beef or pork, saba bananas, potatoes, cabbage, and tomato sauce. It’s hearty, slightly sweet, and rich in flavor.

Why order it in a restaurant:

  • The mix of ingredients requires time and skill to balance flavors.
  • Perfect for sharing—usually served in large portions.
  • Some versions come with chorizo or bone marrow, adding a luxurious touch.

Ingredients:

  • 1 kg pork or beef, cut into chunks
  • 1 liter water
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste (or 1/2 cup tomato sauce)
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 medium potato, cubed
  • 1 large carrot, sliced
  • 2 pieces saba banana, sliced
  • 1 small cabbage, quartered
  • 1 bunch pechay (bok choy)
  • 1 can chickpeas (garbanzos)
  • 1/4 cup fish sauce or soy sauce
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Procedure:

  1. Boil pork or beef in water until tender. Remove scum that rises to the surface.
  2. In a separate pan, heat oil and sauté garlic and onion.
  3. Add the boiled meat and tomato paste. Stir well.
  4. Pour in the broth and bring to a boil.
  5. Add potatoes, carrots, and saba bananas. Cook until tender.
  6. Stir in chickpeas, cabbage, and pechay. Simmer for another 3-5 minutes.
  7. Season with fish sauce, salt, and pepper.
  8. Serve hot with rice.
